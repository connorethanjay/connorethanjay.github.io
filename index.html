The Infrastructure Gap: What Many Education Programs Experience

LIMITED ACCESS

Without dedicated infrastructure, remote students are limited to running virtual machines & other resources on their hardware-limited personal devices, unlike on-campus students who have access to dedicated cyber-ranges & lab environments.

CAPITAL INVESTMENT

Building a physical space or performing renovations to a space in order to host infrastructure could cost your institution millions of dollars in equipment, power, construction, and staffing costs.

CAREER READINESS

Without access to strong hands-on training, learners fall behind, requiring more on-the-job training over their direct competition.

Three-Phase Process

Step 1: We Provision Your Private Cloud

When you partner with LabScale, we allocate dedicated infrastructure for your institution. This is a process that can take time and may be multiple conversations between your team and our team.

An example of what we allocate to you would be a number of physical servers forming their own private-cloud environment, the applicable role-based access for your students & faculty, as well as management access for those who need it. Role-based access for faculty will have permissions to create the environments and virtual machines your students need.

Your infrastructure is completely isolated from other clients by using VRF multi-tenancy. Think of it as your own private datacenter, accessible online.

Timeline: TBD

Step 2: Your Faculty Design New Labs

Once your private-cloud is provisioned, your faculty may request training on how to use the platform, including documentation and best practices. Below are examples of what faculty can do with your newly provisioned private-cloud:

  • Deploy VM templates for student use.
  • Build virtualized networks to match your curriculum (eg. in GNS3).
  • Create isolated course environments.
  • Set up automated VM provisioning.

You maintain 100% control over:

  • Assignment structure, complexity and difficulty
  • Assessment criteria
  • Academic integrity measures

Timeline: Ongoing, evolves with curriculum

Step 3: Students Access the Private-Cloud

Students log into your specific LabScale portal using institutional credentials (SSO integration available). They see VMs assigned by faculty and are able to access those VMs in seconds.

Student Workflow:

  • Open web browser
  • Navigate to your college’s LabScale portal URL (example: example.labscale.io)
  • Log in with your credentials.
  • Students can now access their dedicated VMs.

LabScale will handle escalated technical support cases, Infrastructure uptime and monitoring, Security and isolation enforcement, Capacity management, and Platform updates / maintenance.

Timeline: Semester-long, 24/7 student access

Frequently Asked Questions

Does LabScale provide course content or a curriculum?

No. LabScale provides the infrastructure and dashboard for your institution. Your faculty design the courses, create assignments, and control curriculum. We provide the foundation, you bring the teaching.

How much do LabScale’s services cost?

Pricing starts at $65 per student per semester with 5% & 10% discounts past a certain capacity of students. No upfront capital investment required.

Pilot program participants will receive specialty pricing of $32.50 per student per semester until the Pilot program is complete.

How do students access the private-cloud to work on assignments?

Students navigate to the dashboard (example: https://dash.labscale.io) and then login with their API token. Once logged in, they can access their course virtual machines. No software installation needed.

How much technical expertise do faculty need?

Faculty should have networking/systems administration knowledge to design labs (similar to what they’d need for on-campus labs). If requested, LabScale can provide platform guidance, training, and will handle infrastructure management & monitoring.

Can we customize the infrastructure for our needs?

Yes. During onboarding, we will work directly with you to determine the amount of resources you need & to configure platform services to match your curriculum requirements.

How is our institution’s infrastructure isolated? Is student data secure?

Each partner institution receives a dedicated private-cloud with VRF (Virtual Routing and Forwarding) isolation, just like how an Internet Service Provider (ISP) operates. Your students and labs are completely separated from other institutions at the network layer.

Students cannot access each other’s environments unless specially requested & no student PII is stored on network devices.
